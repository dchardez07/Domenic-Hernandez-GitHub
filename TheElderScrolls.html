The Elder Scrolls Page-Domenic Hernandez

The Elder Scrolls I: Arena

Released in 1994, Arena was the first installment to the Elder Scrolls franchise. Arena was released for MS-DOS and changed the open world/role-playing gaming forever. Throughout the game, the player gets to use weapons and magic to survive. In addition, Arena provided players with an open world map that had never been seen before.

The Elder Scrolls II: Daggerfall

The sequel to Arena, Daggerfall was released in 1996 for MS-DOS. Providing players with a bigger map than Arena, Daggerfall also introduced slightly improved graphics and a new map and enviornment. Players were granted the amazing abilities to become vampires, werewolves, and even wereboars. Bethesda also introduced the ability to purchase houses and ships and included politics between kingdoms as well.

The Elder Scrolls III: Morrowind

Bethesda released Morrowind in 2002, making the game available for Microsoft Windows and XBOX. Morrowind provided players with graphics from another generation and bringing them to a bigger world, placed in the province of Morrowind. The third installment to the series improved its magic and combat system and allowed players to level up and increase skill by training, practice, or study. Morrowind also created new weapons, armor, and spells for the player. Another aspect that Bethesda improved on was the amount of quests and locations. Morrowind was stocked full of quests and locations for players to discover and complete.

The Elder Scrolls IV: Oblivion

The fourth installment to the Elder Scrolls Series was officialy released on March 20, 2006 for Microsoft Windows, Playstation 3, and XBOX 360. Oblivion was shared in its creation by both Bethesda Studios and 2K games. Taking place in the amazing province of Cyrodiil, players are given the ability to fully customize and personalize their characters. Oblivion also divided the games experience into three categories, combat, magic, and stealth. Oblivion, as well as the rest of the Elder Scrolls games, provided players with different skill options divided by the three categories. Throughout the game, the player travels through the Imperial province of Cyrodiil, the heartland of the empire. Oblivion also had much improved graphics, lighting, and settings as well.

The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim

Considered the best in the series and one of the best games ever released, Skyrim created the most intriguing and advanced open world adventure in the Elder Scrolls franchise. Released on November 11, 2011 for Microsoft Windows, Playstation 3, and XBOX 360. Players get to spend their adventure in the nordic province of Skyrim, one of the biggest in Tamriel. Skyrim offers amazing graphics with astounding views and scenery, and a lot more. Taking the role of Dovakhiin, players are given the power of the Thu'um, magical and powerful shouts. Dovakhiin is given the responsibility to save Skyrim and all of Tamriel from the dragons and their leader, Alduin. With hundreds of quests and locations to see, Skyrim gives players the true feeling of the medieval times. With improved and polished combat and magic, Skyrim provides the biggest arsenal of weapons, armor, and spells of the franchise. And with that, Skyrim also came out with three of the biggest official DLCs in the series: Dawngaurd, Hearthfire, and Dragonborn.

The Elder Scrolls Online: Tamriel Online

The Elder Scrolls Online was released by Bethesda Studios in 2014, for Micrososft Windows, Playstation 4, and XBOX One. EO allows players to experience the gameplay and feeling of Skyrim, while playing with friends online. Providing plenty of quests, the game allows players to team up to complete quests and other objectives throughout the game. With amazing and futuristic graphics, Elder Scrolls Online revolutionized the next-gen gaming and the open world/multiplayer gaming forever. And with still more additions to the game, ESO is certainly one of the best games for next-gen consoles.
